Dave Grohl

As drummer for Nirvana and founding member of the Foo Fighters, Dave Grohl gave alternative rock its driving beat.

Time line of Dave Grohl and his currently band Foo Figthers:

  • 1969 - David Eric Grohl was born on January 14, in Warren, Ohio.
  • 1979 - Formed his first band.
  • 1991 - He did get to audition for Nirvana. Their first release with them, “Nevermind”, became a huge hit, driven in part by the single, “Smells Like Teen Spirit”.
  • 1993 - The band made one more studio album together, “In Utero”. Rolling Stone called it “brilliant, corrosive, enraged and thoughtful", most of them all at once.
  • 1994 - After Cobain’s death, the remaining members of Nirvana won a Grammy Award for their live recording on MTV called Unplugged in New York.
  • 1995 - After Nirvana, Grohl formed the Foo Fighters.
  • 1997 - The first Foo Fighters album as a band, The Colour and the Shape, came out.
  • 2000 - Foo Fighters won their first Grammy Award for Best Rock Album.
  • 2007 - Their album Echoes, Silence, Patience and Grace, came out in the fall.
  • 2011 - The group released Wasting Light.
  • 2014 - Released Sonic Highways.
  • 2017 - Came out the album Concrete and Gold.
  • 2018 - Foo Figthers won a Best Rock Song Grammy for "Run".
